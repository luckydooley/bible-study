📌 Week in Review

This week asked one question and spent six days proving the answer is bigger than we imagined: who is the God we are trying to know?

  • ☩Sabbath: To Know God. Jesus defined eternal life not as a destination but as a relationship: knowing the Father and the Son He sent. The Greek ginosko and Hebrew yada set the week's terms: real knowing is experiential and intimate, not intellectual and distant.
  • ☩Sunday: A Clearer Picture of God. The serpent's three-move attack in Eden (question, contradict, redefine motive) exposed the original strategy for distorting God's character, a strategy still running today.
  • ☩Monday: God Is Holy. Holiness is the keystone attribute that makes every other attribute safe. Without it, God's power would be terrifying and His love unreliable. Isaiah 57:15 revealed the paradox: the transcendent God revives the crushed.
  • ☩Tuesday: God Is Love. Love is not something God does; it is who He is. The New Testament essentially reinvented agape to describe a love that had no precedent in human vocabulary, a love proven not at its most convenient but at the cross.
  • ☩Wednesday: God in Creation. Two names, one God. Elohim speaks galaxies into existence; Yahweh kneels to form a human from dust. Transcendence and intimacy are not contradictions; they are complementary dimensions of the same Creator.
  • ☩Thursday: Immanuel, God With Us. Every thread converged in Jesus. The holy, loving, creative God became a human being, not as a visit but as a permanent relocation. Matthew's Gospel opens and closes with the same promise: God is with us.
🗺️ Historical & Cultural Context
Six Days, One Portrait

Every ancient culture had gods. Egypt had hundreds. Mesopotamia had an entire bureaucracy of them. Greece gave its gods human appetites and Roman emperors declared themselves divine. What none of these civilizations produced was a coherent, unified portrait of a single deity who was simultaneously holy, loving, creative, transcendent, intimate, and personally present. The attributes were scattered across pantheons: one god for power, another for fertility, another for war. The idea that all of these qualities could belong to a single being, and that this being wanted to be known, was Israel's radical contribution to human thought.

This week traced that portrait one attribute at a time: holiness on Monday, love on Tuesday, creative power on Wednesday, incarnate presence on Thursday. Each day added a dimension. But the week's arc was not a catalog; it was a narrative. The attributes are not separate bullet points on a divine resume. They are facets of one character, and that character is most fully visible in one Person. The ancient world tried to know its gods through ritual, sacrifice, and divination. Israel was told to know its God through relationship. And then God made that relationship as close as it could possibly get: He moved in.

📖 Deeper Questions

This week covered an enormous amount of ground: from the serpent's lies in Eden to the incarnation in Bethlehem, from the meaning of holiness to the nature of divine love. The questions below are invitations to sit with what landed hardest, to push into what is still unsettled, and to let the week's truths shape something real in your life.

1
As you consider the attributes of God that you studied this week, which one has most impacted your understanding of God?
Psalm 145:3
☩ Open in Bible App
"The LORD is great and is highly praised; his greatness is unsearchable."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
The word "unsearchable" is the key. David is not saying God is unknowable; he is saying God is inexhaustible. You can study His holiness, His love, His creative power, and His presence for a lifetime and still find yourself standing at the edge of something vast. That is not a problem. That is the invitation. The attribute that reshaped your picture of God this week is not the final word; it is a doorway. The more you learn about who He is, the more there is to discover, because He is not a concept to master but a Person to know.
2
What other attributes of God could you study to deepen and strengthen your relationship with Him?
Romans 11:33
☩ Open in Bible App
"Oh, the depth of the riches and the wisdom and the knowledge of God! How unsearchable his judgments and untraceable his ways!"
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Paul's exclamation here comes after eleven chapters of wrestling with God's plan for humanity. It is not a dismissal ("we can never figure God out") but a celebration ("there is always more"). This week covered holiness, love, creative power, and incarnate presence. But God's character also includes His faithfulness, His mercy, His justice, His patience, His wisdom, and His sovereignty. Each one is another window into who He is. The question is not which attribute is most important; it is which one you need most right now. Where is your picture of God still fuzzy? That is where to look next.
3
With a family member or friend, read or listen to Steps to Christ, chapter 1, and discuss it together. What new insights about God's character and Jesus has this chapter made you think about?
Romans 1:20
☩ Open in Bible App
"For his invisible attributes, that is, his eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly seen since the creation of the world, being understood through what he has made."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
The first chapter of Steps to Christ opens with the natural world as evidence of God's love: flowers, birds, sunsets, the intricate beauty woven into creation. Paul makes the same move here: God's invisible character is made visible through what He has made. The chapter expands from nature to Scripture to the cross, building a case that everything in existence is God trying to show us who He is. Reading it with someone else adds a dimension you cannot get alone, because another person will notice what you miss. Their question, their surprise, their pushback becomes part of how God reveals Himself to you through community.
4
Many people have a distorted picture of God, which Jesus came to correct. What can you do to share a clear, accurate picture of God's character to those in your sphere of influence?
2 Corinthians 4:6
☩ Open in Bible App
"For God who said, 'Let light shine out of darkness,' has shone in our hearts to give the light of the knowledge of God's glory in the face of Jesus Christ."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Paul connects creation and redemption in a single sentence: the same God who said "let there be light" has put that light inside believers. Sharing an accurate picture of God starts not with arguments but with reflection. If the light of God's character has shone in your heart, it will show up in how you treat people, how you respond under pressure, and how you talk about Him when no one is grading you. Sunday's study showed that the serpent's strategy is to distort God's character. The counter-strategy is not louder theology; it is a life that accurately represents who God actually is. People believe what they see in you before they believe what they hear from you.
2 Corinthians 3:18
☩ Open in Bible App
"We all, with unveiled faces, are looking as in a mirror at the glory of the Lord and are being transformed into the same image from glory to glory; this is from the Lord who is the Spirit."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
This verse describes the mechanism. When you spend time looking at who God really is, you are gradually transformed into His likeness. The phrase "from glory to glory" implies a process, not an event. You do not need to be a finished product before you share what you have seen. Every step of genuine transformation makes God's character more visible through yours. The clearer your picture of Him becomes, the clearer it becomes to the people around you.
5
Although we are clearly sinful and not holy, the Bible makes clear statements about God's people living holy lives. What does it really mean to live a holy life?
1 Peter 1:15-16
☩ Open in Bible App
"But as the one who called you is holy, you also are to be holy in all your conduct; for it is written, Be holy, because I am holy."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Peter quotes Leviticus 19:2, pulling the command straight from the Torah into the New Testament church. The phrase "in all your conduct" is the crucial qualifier: holiness is not a feeling, a title, or a spiritual resume. It shows up in what you do, how you speak, and how you treat people when it costs you something. Monday's study established that holiness is the keystone attribute that makes every other attribute of God safe. The same principle applies to believers: holiness is what makes your love trustworthy, your power safe, and your influence worth following.
Romans 6:22
☩ Open in Bible App
"But now, since you have been set free from sin and have become enslaved to God, you have your fruit, which results in sanctification -- and the outcome is eternal life!"
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Paul frames holiness as a direction, not a destination you arrive at once. "Results in sanctification" describes a process: the fruit grows over time. Freedom from sin does not mean sinlessness; it means a fundamental change of allegiance. You are no longer owned by the old patterns. The fruit of that freedom, a life that increasingly reflects God's character, is sanctification in real time. This is not perfectionism. This is growth.
Hebrews 12:14
☩ Open in Bible App
"Pursue peace with everyone, and holiness -- without it no one will see the Lord."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
The writer of Hebrews pairs holiness with peace, linking vertical transformation to horizontal relationships. And the verb is "pursue," not "achieve." Holiness is something you chase, actively and intentionally, not something you stumble into or check off a list. The stakes are real: "without it no one will see the Lord." Not because holiness earns access to God, but because without the desire to become more like Him, you will not recognize Him when you see Him. Living a holy life means wanting to reflect who God is so deeply that it changes how you treat your neighbor, how you handle failure, and how you spend your ordinary Tuesday afternoons.
🌎 Still Speaking
The Knowing That Never Finishes

Neuroscience has confirmed something that poets and theologians have said for centuries: deep knowing changes you physically. A growing body of research on neuroplasticity, including a 2025 overview published by the University of California, shows that when you learn something new, your brain does not simply file the information away. It rewires itself. New neural pathways form, existing connections strengthen, and the very structure of your brain adapts to what you are giving it. The more you engage with a subject, the more capacity you build to understand it. Knowing is not a file you download once. It is a living process that reshapes the knower.

That finding maps remarkably well onto what this week has been about. Knowing God is not a course you complete. It is a relationship that deepens precisely because there is always more to discover. The paternal love that has flowed through human history, generation to generation, all the springs of tenderness that have opened in human hearts, are a tiny rill compared to the boundless ocean of God's love. You can meditate on it every day of your life. You can summon every capacity you have to comprehend the love of a Father who gave His Son to die for the world. And there will still be infinity beyond. That is not frustrating. That is the design.

The four Gospels themselves illustrate the principle. Matthew shows Jesus as King. Mark shows Jesus as Servant. Luke shows Jesus as Healer. John shows Jesus as the eternal Word. Four writers, four angles, four portraits of the same Person, and still no single account could contain Him. Eternity itself will not exhaust what there is to know about who God is. But the journey of discovering Him, the daily act of looking into His Word and letting it look into you, rewires something deeper than neurons. It transforms the soul, "from glory to glory," as Paul wrote, into the image of the One you are learning to see.

✝️ Christ at the Center of the Week

Every day this week was a different window, but every window opened onto the same Person.

On Sabbath, Jesus defined eternal life as knowing the Father and the Son He sent. That was the invitation. On Sunday, the serpent's three-move attack on God's character in Eden found its answer in Philippians 2:6-7: Jesus did not consider equality with God something to exploit. He emptied Himself. Every lie the enemy tells about God's motives, Jesus refutes by His life. On Monday, holiness met us in Isaiah 57:15, the high and exalted One who revives the crushed. Then the holy God walked among sinners, touched lepers, and ate with outcasts, not because holiness was compromised but because holiness came to heal. On Tuesday, love was revealed as God's nature, not just His action. Romans 5:8 put a date and an address on that love: "while we were still sinners, Christ died for us." On Wednesday, the transcendent Elohim who spoke galaxies into being and the intimate Yahweh who shaped dust with His hands turned out to be the same God. And on Thursday, those two names met in one cradle. John 1:14: the Word became flesh and tabernacled among us.

The week's question was "who is the God we are trying to know?" The answer, every single day, was Jesus. He is the holiness you can trust, the love you can stand in, the Creator who knelt to wash feet, and the God who did not send a message but came Himself. If you want to know what God is like, look at Him. John 14:9 still stands: "The one who has seen Me has seen the Father."

Start tonight. Let the knowing continue.
🙏 Closing Prayer for the Week

Father, this week You showed us who You are, and what we found was bigger than we expected. You are holy, and that holiness is what makes You safe. You are love, not as a mood but as the core of Your being, proven at the cross. You are the Creator whose power built the cosmos and whose hands shaped the first human from dust. And then You came. You did not stay distant. You entered what You made, lived what we live, and gave what it cost to bring us home. Forgive us for the distorted pictures we have carried, the secondhand versions of You that we never thought to question. Keep reshaping our understanding. Keep showing us more. We have barely scratched the surface, and we would not want it any other way. In Jesus' name, Amen.
